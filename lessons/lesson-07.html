💬 Dialogue - At a Clothing Store

店员:
您好，想买什么？
Nín hǎo, xiǎng mǎi shénme?
Hello, what would you like to buy?
你:
我想看看这件衣服。
Wǒ xiǎng kànkan zhè jiàn yīfu.
I'd like to look at this shirt.
店员:
好的，这是新款。您穿多大？
Hǎo de, zhè shì xīnkuǎn. Nín chuān duōdà?
OK, this is new style. What size do you wear?
你:
中号。多少钱？
Zhōnghào. Duōshao qián?
Medium. How much?
店员:
一百五十块。
Yì bǎi wǔshí kuài.
150 Yuan.
你:
太贵了，便宜一点儿吧？
Tài guì le, piányi yìdiǎnr ba?
Too expensive, can it be cheaper?
店员:
好吧，一百二十块。
Hǎo ba, yì bǎi èrshí kuài.
OK, 120 Yuan.
你:
好的，我要了。
Hǎo de, wǒ yào le.
OK, I'll take it.

📝 Grammar Points

1. 多少钱？(How much?)

Used to ask about price. Example: 这个多少钱？(How much is this?)

2. 太 + adjective + 了

Expresses "too" or "so" with emphasis.
太贵了！= Too expensive!
太便宜了！= So cheap!
太好了！= Great! / That's too good!

3. Verb + 一点儿 (a little bit)

Asks for a slight change.
便宜一点儿 = a little cheaper
快一点儿 = a little faster

💡 Bargaining Tips in China

In markets and small shops, bargaining is common! Start at 50-70% of the asking price. In department stores and supermarkets, prices are fixed.
