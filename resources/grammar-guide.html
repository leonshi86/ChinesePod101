📖 Chinese Grammar Guide

Essential grammar patterns for beginners

1. Basic Sentence Structure

Beginner

Subject + Verb + Object

Chinese follows the same basic word order as English.

Subject + Verb + Object
我 吃 苹果。
Wǒ chī píngguǒ.
I eat apples.
他 学 中文。
Tā xué Zhōngwén.
He studies Chinese.

2. Questions

Beginner

吗 (ma) Questions

Add 吗 at the end of a statement to make it a yes/no question.

Statement + 吗？
你好吗？
Nǐ hǎo ma?
Are you well?
你是中国人吗？
Nǐ shì Zhōngguórén ma?
Are you Chinese?
Beginner

什么 (shénme) - What

... + 什么？
这是什么？
Zhè shì shénme?
What is this?
你叫什么名字？
Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
What is your name?
Beginner

几 (jǐ) / 多少 (duōshao) - How many/much

几 is used for small numbers (under 10), 多少 for larger numbers.

你几岁？
Nǐ jǐ suì?
How old are you? (for children)
这个多少钱？
Zhège duōshao qián?
How much is this?

3. Measure Words

Beginner

Number + Measure Word + Noun

Chinese requires measure words between numbers and nouns.

Number + 个 (gè) + Noun
一个人
yí gè rén
one person
三个苹果
sān gè píngguǒ
three apples

Common measure words:

  • 个 (gè) - general
  • 只 (zhī) - animals
  • 本 (běn) - books
  • 杯 (bēi) - cups
  • 碗 (wǎn) - bowls

4. 了 (le) - Past Tense

Beginner

Verb + 了

Use 了 to indicate completed action.

Subject + Verb + 了 + Object
我吃了早饭。
Wǒ chī le zǎofàn.
I ate breakfast.
他去了北京。
Tā qù le Běijīng.
He went to Beijing.

5. 不 (bù) - Negation

Beginner

不 + Verb/Adjective

Use 不 to negate verbs and adjectives.

Subject + 不 + Verb/Adjective
我不吃肉。
Wǒ bù chī ròu.
I don't eat meat.
这个不好吃。
Zhège bù hǎochī.
This is not delicious.

6. 的 (de) - Possession

Beginner

Possessor + 的 + Noun

Use 的 to show possession (like 's in English).

A + 的 + B (A's B)
我的书
wǒ de shū
my book
中国的食物
Zhōngguó de shíwù
Chinese food
